Full 12-layer sweeps, wqk + wov circuit tensors (768×768×12), matched-parameter
comparison, permutation-null control (`a2_matched_param/20260610-225704`,
`a3_permutation_null/20260610-231318`, figure `error_vs_params.png`).

- **BMD is the worst method at every matched budget.** Median rel_error across
  layers, wqk: BMD ℓ=8 (4.87M params) → 0.33 vs Tucker (512,512,12) (3.93M) →
  0.127; CP-512 (0.79M) → 0.32; slice-SVD is EXACT at 1.18M (slices have rank

- **The real-vs-null differential isolates the verdict.** Tucker improves

  vs 0.189 above): genuine cross-slice structure exists and is
  **subspace-shaped**. BMD's real-vs-null gap is ≈0 (0.759 vs 0.783): no
  diag-template alignment beyond chance. This is exactly the discriminator's
  discard branch (Tucker finds structure, BM does not).
- Solver validity is not in question: the same RALS beats the BM-ALS paper's
  solver by 3–10 orders of magnitude on its own benchmark tensors
  (`tests/test_sagemath_agreement.py`), and CPU-lstsq vs GPU-ridge agree at
  layer 0 (0.200@20it vs 0.187@200it).

Per the research plan's gate: discarded for attention; **Track C (fine-grained
MoE experts) remains open** — different object, different redundancy prior.

## Track B (b1 + ncu): bytes mechanism CONFIRMED; realization needs a kernel

`b1_kernel_bench/20260610-224252` (fp32, 720 cases), `…-224846` (fp16),
ncu profiles `ncu-20260610-225349` (d=4096 h=64 ℓ=2), `…-225555` (d=768 h=12 ℓ=2).

- **Hardware byte counters match the model exactly.** Dense GEMV reads
  4,295 MB/launch ≡ h·m·p·4B; the bmm template GEMM reads 136 MB/launch ≡
  ℓ·m·p·4B → **32× fewer DRAM bytes** at h=64, ℓ=2. Same at GPT-2 scale
  (28.3 vs 4.8 MB). Dense baseline runs at full ~3.2 TB/s (memory-bound
  regime confirmed).
- **Wall-clock captures only part of it.** batch=1, d=4096, fp32: ℓ=1 →
  3.5–19×; ℓ=2 → 1.1–3.5× (the original einsum path LOST at ℓ≥2 — fixed by
  the pre-transposed-template bmm variant). The gap to the bytes-ideal is
  kernel utilization: the skinny batched GEMM achieves ~10% of peak bandwidth
  → **~9× headroom for a fused (Triton) kernel**, template-stationary with
  gains fused into prologue/epilogue (no online-softmax-style trick needed;
  the reduction is linear).
- **The win decays with batch** (dense amortizes its own reads): fp16 ℓ=2
  h=64: 3.2× @ batch 1 → 1.1× @ batch 32. The factored form is a small-batch
  (decode / expert-streaming) play, as the bandwidth-amplification framing
  implies.
- d=768 is launch-overhead-bound (3 launches vs 1): torch-level composition
  cannot win at GPT-2 scale; only a fused kernel could.

## Late-session addendum: Tracks C1 + C2 (same instance)

**C1 census** (`c1_redundancy_census/`, OLMoE + Qwen1.5-MoE + DeepSeek-V2-Lite):
all three fine-grained checkpoints show the same signature — experts are
orthogonal as flat weight vectors (cos ~ 0, zero mergeable pairs) but share
substantial global second-moment structure (mean off-diag CKA 0.25-0.38,
participation ratio ~ 0.15*E => ~10 global modes). Redundancy is global, not
clustered: failure mode 2 not triggered, gate said proceed.

**C2 discriminator** (`c2_expert_structure/`, OLMoE layers 0/7/15, gate+down,
matched parameters): the global structure is too thin to exploit. At 27% of
dense params the best method reaches only RE ~ 0.73-0.75; at streaming-relevant
budgets (<=10%) everything is 0.93-0.99. BMD never separates from Tucker or
slice-SVD at any matched budget (e.g. 18M params: 0.874 vs 0.878). Entry 2's
falsifiable claim (BM-rank <= E/8 meaningfully below per-expert SVD/Tucker)
is **falsified on OLMoE**: at ell = E/8 = 8, RE ~ 0.86-0.87, indistinguishable
from baselines.

**Program-level conclusion:** the diag-template prior does not describe
trained transformer weight stacks — neither attention circuits nor
fine-grained MoE experts. Entries 1 and 2 are discarded with measured reasons;
what survives the session is the Track B kernel result (bytes mechanism
confirmed exactly by ncu; h/ell realizable with kernel work — applicable if a
template-shaped object is ever found), the C1 census finding (global thin
second-moment sharing across experts — interesting in its own right), the d1
Gaussianization evidence, and entry 3's re-scoped unbiased-matvec cluster.
